# SKILL: EU Pharmacovigilance Section Analyzer

## Purpose
Analyze pharmacovigilance-related sections in EU regulatory submissions (MAAs, variations, renewals, PSURs, RMPs, PASS protocols) against EMA Good Pharmacovigilance Practices (GVP) requirements from Modules I, II, V, VII, VIII, IX, XV, and XVI.

## When to Use
- Reviewing the pharmacovigilance section of an EU marketing authorisation application
- Evaluating an EU Risk Management Plan (RMP) for completeness
- Assessing a PSUR or PSUR EU regional appendix
- Reviewing a PASS protocol or final study report
- Evaluating safety communication materials (DHPCs)
- Checking risk minimisation measures (RMM) for compliance
- Auditing a Pharmacovigilance System Master File (PSMF)
- Comparing EU PV requirements against FDA equivalents

## Legal Framework
- **Regulation (EC) No 726/2004** (REG) — centrally authorised products
- **Directive 2001/83/EC** (DIR) — nationally authorised products
- **Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) No 520/2012** (IR) — PV procedures
- Requirements use **"shall"** = legal obligation, **"should"** = recommendation

## Analysis Framework

### 1. PHARMACOVIGILANCE SYSTEM (Module I)

**Check for:**
- [ ] QPPV designated, EU-resident, continuously available
- [ ] QPPV has sufficient authority over PV system decisions
- [ ] Quality system covers: organisational structure, responsibilities, procedures, processes, resources
- [ ] Document management with version control
- [ ] Training system with records of PV staff qualifications
- [ ] Audit programme with CAPA tracking
- [ ] Record management ensuring retrievability and traceability
- [ ] Written procedures for all PV activities
- [ ] Back-up arrangements for QPPV

**Key obligations:**
- QPPV shall have oversight of safety profile and proposed risk management measures
- PV system shall be regularly audited with documented findings and CAPA
- All PV staff shall be appropriately qualified, trained, and experienced

### 2. PSMF (Module II)

**Mandatory PSMF contents:**
- [ ] QPPV details (name, contact, CV, qualifications)
- [ ] Organisational structure for PV
- [ ] Data sources and systems description
- [ ] Computerised systems validation status
- [ ] Written procedures list
- [ ] Quality system description
- [ ] List of medicinal products covered
- [ ] Performance indicators (ICSR compliance, timeliness KPIs)
- [ ] Audit schedule and findings summary
- [ ] Logbook of significant changes
- [ ] List of delegated tasks with contractual partners

**Compliance checks:**
- PSMF location notified to competent authorities
- Available within 7 calendar days for inspection
- Logbook maintained with all significant changes documented
- Performance indicators tracked and reported in annexes

### 3. RISK MANAGEMENT PLAN (Module V)

**RMP structure (EU-RMP format):**
- [ ] Part I: Product Overview
- [ ] Part II: Safety Specification
  - Module SI: Epidemiology of indications and target populations
  - Module SII: Non-clinical safety
  - Module SIII: Clinical trial exposure
  - Module SIV: Populations not studied in clinical trials
  - Module SV: Post-authorisation experience
  - Module SVI: Additional EU requirements for safety specification
  - Module SVII: Identified and potential risks
  - Module SVIII: Summary of safety concerns
- [ ] Part III: Pharmacovigilance Plan (with milestones)
- [ ] Part IV: Plans for PASS (Categories 1, 2, 3)
- [ ] Part V: Risk Minimisation Measures (routine + additional)
- [ ] Part VI: Summary of RMP
- [ ] Part VII: Annexes

**Safety specification must include:**
- Identified risks (established causal association)
- Potential risks (signals, non-clinical findings)
- Missing information (gaps in knowledge)
- Summary table characterising each concern

**Study categories to verify:**
- Category 1: Imposed as obligation [REG Art 9(4)(cb), 10a(1)(a)]
- Category 2: Condition of MA under exceptional circumstances
- Category 3: Required in RMP/specific obligation

### 4. PSUR (Module VII)

**PSUR format (ICH E2C(R2) + EU appendix):**
- [ ] Sections 1-18 per ICH E2C(R2)
- [ ] EU Regional Appendix containing:
  - Proposed product information changes (SmPC/PL track changes)
  - Proposed additional PV and risk minimisation activities
  - Summary of ongoing safety concerns (RMP Table 1.10)
  - Reporting of PASS results (interventional + non-interventional)
  - Effectiveness of risk minimisation (EU-specific evaluation)

**Submission compliance:**
- Data lock point per EURD list or IBD
- Submission within 90 calendar days of DLP
- EURD list checked for frequency and reference dates
- Fixed-dose combinations: separate PSUR or data within single-substance PSUR

**Assessment procedure:**
- Single centrally authorised product: Figure VII.6 (60+30+15 day cycle)
- EU single assessment (multiple MAHs, same substance): Figure VII.7
- PRAC recommendation → CHMP opinion (CAPs) or CMDh position (non-CAPs)
- Regulatory outcomes are legally binding

### 5. PASS (Module VIII)

**Protocol requirements (IR Annex III format):**
1. Title (study design, product/class, version, date)
2. Marketing authorisation holder
3. Responsible parties (PI, investigators, affiliations)
4. Abstract (standalone, 12 sub-sections)
5. Amendments and updates
6. Milestones table (start/end data collection, progress reports, interim reports, final report)
7. Rationale and background
8. Research question and objectives
9. Research methods (9 sub-sections: design, setting, variables, data sources, study size, data management, data analysis, quality control, limitations)
10. Protection of human subjects
11. AE/ADR management and reporting
12. Dissemination and communication plans
13. References

**Registration:**
- Register in EU PAS Register BEFORE study commencement
- Protocol uploaded prior to data collection start
- Updates for substantial amendments uploaded within 2 weeks
- Final study report uploaded as soon as possible after finalisation

**Key constraints:**
- Study shall NOT promote use of medicinal product
- HCP payments limited to compensation for time and expenses
- QPPV shall review/sign-off study protocols
- Final report within 12 months of end of data collection
- Imposed PASS: PRAC endorsement required before commencement (60-day review)

**Final study report (IR Annex III format):**
14 mandatory sections including abstract format with 13 sub-items

### 6. SIGNAL MANAGEMENT (Module IX)

**Signal management process:**
1. Signal detection → 2. Signal validation → 3. Signal confirmation (PRAC/Lead MS, 30 days) → 4. Signal analysis & prioritisation → 5. Signal assessment → 6. Recommendation for action

**MAH obligations:**
- [ ] Continuously monitor product safety
- [ ] Monitor EudraVigilance data (≥ every 6 months; more frequently for additional monitoring products)
- [ ] Validate detected signals with thorough EudraVigilance + own database analysis
- [ ] Report emerging safety issues within 3 working days
- [ ] Document signal management process in PSMF
- [ ] Keep product information up to date based on signal findings

**Signal evaluation criteria (IX.B.3):**
- Previous awareness (SmPC, RMP, PSUR)
- Strength of evidence (case count, temporal association, dechallenge/rechallenge, dose-response, biological plausibility, disproportionality)
- Clinical relevance (seriousness, severity, outcome, reversibility, vulnerable populations, patterns of use)

**Signal notification options:**
1. Variation application (3 months for important risks, 6 months for non-important)
2. PSUR inclusion (if EURD-listed and PSUR due within 6 months)
3. Standalone signal notification (within 30 days of completing assessment)

**Emerging safety issues — notify via:**
- Email to P-PV-emerging-safety-issue@ema.europa.eu
- Written notification to NCAs of Member States where authorised
- Include: safety issue description, source(s), planned/taken actions, timelines, documentation

### 7. SAFETY COMMUNICATION (Module XV)

**DHPC triggers (mandatory):**
- Suspension/withdrawal/revocation of MA for safety
- Important change to use (indication restriction, new contraindication, dose change)
- Restriction in availability with detrimental patient effects

**DHPC triggers (consider):**
- New warnings/precautions in product information
- Previously unknown risk or changed frequency/severity
- Evidence of reduced effectiveness
- New recommendations for preventing/treating adverse reactions
- Ongoing assessment of important potential risk

**DHPC template (GVP Annex II):**
- Summary (bold/larger font, bullet points)
- Background on safety concern
- Call for reporting (reminder of spontaneous reporting)
- Company contact point
- Annexes (references, additional scientific info)

**Communication plan template:**
- Medicinal product/active substance
- MAH(s) and contact point arrangements
- Safety concern and purpose description
- DHPC recipients (by HCP type)
- Member States for distribution
- Timetable (PRAC agreement → CHMP/CMDh → translations → NCA review → dissemination)

**Coordination requirements:**
- 24-hour advance notification via Early Notification System (ENS)
- Member States shall agree on common message and timetable
- Translations submitted for NCA review (4-5 working days)
- Member States aim to review translations within 48 hours
- Safety announcements shared with international partners under embargo

### 8. RISK MINIMISATION MEASURES (Module XVI)

**Routine RMM tools:**
| Tool | Description |
|------|-------------|
| SmPC | Fundamental RMM tool — risk descriptions and intended actions |
| Package leaflet | Patient-facing risk information |
| Labelling | Special warnings, pictograms on packaging |
| Pack size | Implicit risk message (e.g., restricted pack = overdose risk) |
| Legal status | Prescription-only, special/restricted prescription |

**Additional RMM — Educational/Safety Advice tools:**
| Tool | Target |
|------|--------|
| Guides for risk minimisation | Patients or HCPs |
| HCP checklist for risk minimisation | HCPs |
| Risk awareness dialogue form/aid | HCPs (for patient discussion) |
| Patient card | Patients |
| Patient diary for risk minimisation | Patients |

**Additional RMM — Risk Minimisation Control tools:**
| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| HCP qualification requirement | Control prescribing/dispensing/administration |
| Facility accreditation | Ensure equipped healthcare settings |
| Traceability system | Track product from manufacture to administration |
| Documented information exchange | Formal HCP-to-HCP patient data sharing |
| Patient certificate check | Verify patient eligibility/testing before prescribing |

**RMM material requirements:**
- RMM tool name as heading
- Product name (invented name + active substance in brackets)
- Invented name used as rarely as possible in body text
- No MAH/product logos (or only once, not larger than heading font)
- Competent authority approval statement with version and "<month> <year>"
- Reference to SmPC/PL
- Non-promotional content only
- GDPR-compliant data handling

**Effectiveness evaluation framework:**
1. **Dissemination outcomes** (12-24 months): coverage, receipt by target populations
2. **Knowledge/behavioural outcomes**: awareness, understanding, attitude formation, intended actions taken
3. **Health outcomes** (within 4 years): reduced occurrence/severity of adverse reactions
4. **Unintended outcomes**: switching, spill-over, non-treatment, lack of adherence, additional prescribing

## EU vs FDA Quick Comparison

| Aspect | EU (GVP) | US (FDA) |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Named PV person | QPPV (mandatory, EU-resident) | Safety reporting contact (less prescriptive) |
| System documentation | PSMF (7-day availability) | No equivalent master file |
| Risk management | RMP for all new MAs | REMS only for high-risk products |
| Periodic reports | PSUR with PRAC single assessment | PBRER largely eliminated |
| Safety studies | PASS with EU PAS Register | PMR/PMC (no public register) |
| Signal management | MAH monitors EudraVigilance | FDA monitors FAERS internally |
| Safety comms | DHPC with 24hr network coordination | Dear HCP letters (simpler) |
| Risk minimisation | Structured RMM taxonomy + effectiveness framework | REMS elements (less granular) |
| Standing committee | PRAC (continuous oversight) | Ad hoc advisory committees |
| Transparency | Published recommendations in all EU languages | Less structured transparency |

## Red Flags Checklist

When reviewing any EU PV submission, flag these issues:

1. **No QPPV designated** or QPPV not EU-resident
2. **PSMF not maintained** or missing logbook/performance indicators
3. **RMP missing** for new MA application or missing any of Parts I-VII
4. **Safety specification** lacking identified/potential risk categorisation or missing information analysis
5. **PSUR** missing EU regional appendix or proposed product information changes
6. **PASS protocol** not registered in EU PAS Register or missing IR Annex III format sections
7. **Imposed PASS** commenced without PRAC written endorsement
8. **Signal management** not documented in PSMF or EudraVigilance monitoring not performed
9. **Emerging safety issue** not notified within 3 working days
10. **DHPC** disseminated without NCA/PRAC agreement or missing GVP Annex II template elements
11. **RMM materials** containing promotional elements or missing competent authority approval statement
12. **RMM effectiveness** not evaluated within prescribed timelines (12-24 months initial, 4 years overall)
13. **Study conduct** promoting the use of the medicinal product
14. **HCP payments** for PASS exceeding compensation for time and expenses
15. **Product information** not updated following validated signal or PRAC recommendation

## Usage Instructions

To use this skill, provide:
1. **Document type**: RMP, PSUR, PASS protocol, DHPC, PSMF, or general PV submission
2. **Product details**: Active substance, authorisation procedure (centralised/MRP/DCP/national)
3. **Specific concern**: What aspect needs analysis (completeness, compliance, comparison)

The analyzer will cross-reference the submission against applicable GVP module requirements and flag gaps, non-compliance, and areas needing attention.
