# UpCommander Benchmark Data

Summary of 52+ controlled benchmark runs on a real production codebase (Next.js / TypeScript / Supabase, ~600K tokens). Full narrative: [upgpt.ai/blog/upcommander-benchmarks](https://upgpt.ai/blog/upcommander-benchmarks).

All runs: Sonnet 4.6 worker, Opus 4.7 grader (validated cross-vendor, ±1 pt spread). Retest on Sonnet/Opus successor models when available.

---

## Finding 1 — CONTRACT.md is the single largest lever (N=20, 2×2 factorial)

| Condition | Cost | Time | Quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline (no contract) | — | — | 5/10 |
| +CONTRACT.md | **−54%** | **−60%** | **9/10** |

Architecture was secondary. Model and codebase held constant.

---

## Finding 2 — Agent Teams cost 73–124% more with zero quality gain (N=5)

| Task size | Sequential + CONTRACT | Agent Teams | Premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| T3 (medium) | baseline | +124% | No quality gain |
| T6 (large) | baseline | +73% | No quality gain |

Each agent loads the full codebase context independently. Cache burn dominates.

---

## Finding 3 — Retry loops degrade quality (N=5)

| Harness | ACs | Quality | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| V2 (no retry) | 5/5 | 9/10 | baseline |
| NS (self-evolution retry) | 4/5 | 6/10 | 2.1× |

Models regenerate whole files on retry, losing previously-correct sections.

---

## Finding 4 — Opus one-shot review adds nothing when contract is good (N=5 clean retest)

| Condition | Quality | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| V2 (Sonnet only, good contract) | 9.8/10 | baseline |
| V2O (Sonnet + Opus one-shot) | 9.8/10 | +56% |

Zero quality gain. Write a better brief; don't pay for a review.

---

## Finding 5 — AST compression: 91% token reduction (28 production files)

Tree-sitter parsing, export-only extraction, cached by git blob SHA. Adapted from [agora-code](https://github.com/thebnbrkr/agora-code).

Stacked with CONTRACT.md on a large T6 session: **$5.45 → $0.85 (−85%)**. Zero quality tradeoff.

---

## Finding 6 — Haiku implements contracts but can't author them (V4, N=3)

| Condition | Contract author | Worker | $/run | Time | Quality |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A (no contract) | — | Sonnet | $1.18 | 252s | 8.5/10 |
| B (spec-derived) | Sonnet | Sonnet | $0.67 | 138s | 7.8/10 |
| E (3× Haiku workers + Sonnet contract) | Sonnet | 3× Haiku | $0.67 | 104s | **7.9/10** |
| G (3× Haiku workers + Haiku contract) | Haiku | 3× Haiku | $0.42 | 75s | **4.9/10** |

**Rule: Sonnet authors, Haiku implements.** Never let Haiku write contracts for multi-file work. All-Haiku is *not* the cost play it looks like in isolation.

---

## Finding 7 — Cross-vendor grading agrees ±1 pt

Same 5 V2 outputs graded by Opus, GPT-4o, Gemini. Spread: ±1.0 pts. Opus is directionally reliable; Gemini is strictest.

---

## Finding 8 — L0 / L1 / L2 tree index

| Level | File | Size | When to load |
|---|---|---|---|
| **L0** | `.codebase-index-l0.json` | ~18KB | Always first |
| **L1** | `.codebase-index/<slug>.json` | 2–3KB each | Per relevant module |
| **L2** | raw source | varies | Only when behavior matters |

Replaces 50–200K tokens of blind Glob/Grep with ~4K tokens of L0 + targeted L1. Generated by a tree-sitter one-shot; pre-commit hook keeps it fresh.

---

## Finding 9 — L1 for discovery, L2 for integration (V5, N=1 directional)

Narrow A/B on a platform event-bus task (10 ACs, spans 6 files):

| Condition | Context | ACs | Quality | Integration | Total | Time |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **B** | L0 + targeted raw files | 10/10 | **8.7/10** | **9.0** | $2.67 | 517s |
| **C** | L0 + L1 modules only | 10/10 | 7.7/10 | 8.0 | **$1.59** | **303s** |

Both conditions passed every AC. B scored higher on integration correctness because the worker saw actual implementations — it wired into the correct existing helpers. C produced working but slightly off-spec integration (used a similarly-named-but-wrong helper because L1 gave it shape without showing which of several candidates was load-bearing).

**N=1 early learning; full N=5 rerun queued.** Mechanism is clear: L1 captures shape, not behavior.

Sub-finding: sequential workers hit **98.1% cache read** on the second-stage call (4.97M of 5.07M input tokens from cache). Sequential beats parallel on cost because the next worker inherits the prompt the previous one warmed.

---

## Replication

- Model: Sonnet 4.6 worker, Opus 4.7 grader
- Codebase: production Next.js / TypeScript / Supabase monorepo, ~600K tokens of source (anonymised for the write-up)
- Task class: greenfield TypeScript + SQL migrations, 3–8 file scope

Your numbers will differ. The *directions* (contracts help, retries hurt, trees beat flat dumps) appear structurally sound; magnitudes are setup-specific. Contradictory findings welcome: [hello@upgpt.ai](mailto:hello@upgpt.ai).
